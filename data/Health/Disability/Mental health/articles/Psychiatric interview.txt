The psychiatric interview refers to the set of tools that a mental health worker (most times a psychiatrist or a psychologist but at times social workers or nurses) uses to complete a psychiatric assessment. As such, one the interview's goals is to collect data that is both valid and reliable.  Validity refers to how the data compares to an ideal absolute truth that the interviewer needs to access and uncover.
