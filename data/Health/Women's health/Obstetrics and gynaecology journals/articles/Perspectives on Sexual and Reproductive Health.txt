Perspectives on Sexual and Reproductive Health is a bimonthly peer-reviewed medical journal covering reproductive health. It was established in 1969 as Family Planning Perspectives, obtaining its current name in 2002.   It is published by Wiley-Blackwell on behalf of the Guttmacher Institute. The editor-in-chief is Dore Hollander. According to the Journal Citation Reports, the journal has a 2016 impact factor of 3.571, ranking it 1st out of 26 journals in the category "Demography" and 1st out of 43 journals in the category "Family studies."
