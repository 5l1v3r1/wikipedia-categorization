Contraceptive use is important to slow population growth as well as a reduction in neonatal mortality, maternal mortality and adverse perinatal outcomes. Most of the previous reports demonstrate that contraceptive use is low in Bangladesh; [full citation needed] however, a recent study found contraceptive prevalence rate 81.27%. It was found that Contraceptive use was affected by contextual factors along with individual and fertility related factors. Visits by a family planning worker (FPW) within the previous six months had the biggest impact on contraceptive use. Education level (adjusted and number of children who are alive were also positively associated with contraceptive use. Contraceptive use was lower among Muslims, desire for a son, living in rural area and Sylhet Division.
