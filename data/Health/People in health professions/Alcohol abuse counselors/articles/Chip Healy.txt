Chip Healy is a former professional American football player who played linebacker for the St. Louis Cardinals.  After retiring from football in 1970, Healy moved around Tennessee working for his father's brokerage business, before retiring in 1987.  Since 2001, he has operated Transitional Living in Nashville, Tennessee, known as "Chip's Place", a treatment and living facility for men struggling with alcoholism,  which initially included Healy himself. Healy is a devout Christian, and lives in Nashville with his dog Willie. He has two children.  His nephew, Will Healy is the head football coach at Austin Peay State University.
