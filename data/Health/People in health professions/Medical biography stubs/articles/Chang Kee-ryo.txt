Chang Kee-ryo (14 August 1911 – 25 December 1995) was a prominent surgeon, educator, and philanthropist of South Korea.   He is the 1979  recipient of the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service and established Gospel hospital, in Busan, Korea, in 1951, during the Korean war.   In 1979, he won the Ramon Magsaysay Award "for his practical, personal Christian charity and in founding the Blue Cross Health Cooperative in Pusan." He was described as a person who tirelessly worked for those in need.  *Chang, Kee Ryo; Yang, Duck Ho/Park, Yong Hoon. (1963). Clinical Study of Primary Cancer of The Extrahepatic Biliary Tract.
