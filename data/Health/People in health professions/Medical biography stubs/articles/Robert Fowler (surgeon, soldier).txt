Robert Fowler (1888–1965) was an Australian surgeon and soldier. He served in World War I with the Australian Mounted Division,  obtaining the rank of Colonel.  In civilian life he practiced at The Alfred Hospital, Melbourne;  represented the Victoria Branch of the British Medical Association;  was an advocate of the ‘Airway Ambulance’ (forerunner to the Royal Flying Doctor Service of Australia);   served on the staff of the Governor-General Sir Isaac Isaacs;  researched the use of the Radium Ray for cancer treatment;  and pioneered the linkage between smoking and lung cancer.
