Sara Hestrin-Lerner (Hebrew: שרה הסטרין-לרנר) (May 1, 1918 – November 18, 2017) was a Canadian-born Israeli physiologist. Hestrin-Lerner was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada in May 1918. Aged 14, she emigrated with her parents to the then British Mandate of Palestine (now Israel) in 1932. She studied zoology  and received her doctorate in  pathological physiology from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.  She died in November 2017 at the age of 99.  In 1955, Hestrin-Lerner was awarded the Israel Prize, for medical science.  Hestrin-Lerner's brother, Shlomo Hestrin, was also awarded the Israel Prize, in exact sciences, in 1957.
