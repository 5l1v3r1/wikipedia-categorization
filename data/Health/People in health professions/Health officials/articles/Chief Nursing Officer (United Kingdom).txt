The Chief Nursing Officer (CNO) is the most senior advisor on nursing matters in a government. There are CNOs in the United Kingdom who are appointed to advise their respective governments: Her Majesty's Government, the Northern Ireland Executive, the Scottish Government, and the Welsh Government. Each CNO is assisted by one or more Deputy Chief Nursing Officers, and are complimented by a Chief Medical Officer. The Chief Nursing Officer is based at the Department of Health (and its predecessors). The Chief Nursing Officers for Scotland is based at the Scottish Government and previously at the Scottish Office. The Chief Nursing Officer for Northern Ireland is based at the Department of Health (Northern Ireland) (and its processors).
