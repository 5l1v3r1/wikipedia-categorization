  The Central Council of Homoeopathy Act 1973, (Act 59), also called the Homoeopathy Central Council Act, 1973, is an Act of the Parliament of India to primarily structure the role of the Central Council of Homoeopathy and to enable the regularization of the maintenance of a central register of issues and entities related to the field of homoeopathy.  It included five chapters when it was initially passed.  The Act was amended in 2002, and the amendment—Homoeopathy Central Council Amendment Act, 2002 (No. 51 of 2002)—was passed in December 2002.
