Workmen's Compensation (Occupational Diseases) Convention, 1925 is  an International Labour Organization Convention. It was established in 1925: Having decided upon the adoption of certain proposals with regard to workmen's compensation for occupational diseases,...This Convention was subsequently revised in 1934 by Convention C42 - Workmen's Compensation (Occupational Diseases) Convention (Revised), 1934, and again in 1964 by Convention C121 - Employment Injury Benefits Convention, 1964. As of 2013, the convention had been ratified by 68 states. Of the ratifying states, eight had subsequently denounced the treaty.
