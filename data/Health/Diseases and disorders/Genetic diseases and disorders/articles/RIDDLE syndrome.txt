RIDDLE syndrome is a rare genetic syndrome. The name is an acronym for Radiosensitivity, ImmunoDeficiency Dysmorphic features and LEarning difficulties. The features of this condition include This condition is due to mutations in the RNF168 gene. It is inherited in an autosomal recessive fashion. The gene encodes a ubiquitin ligase and is located on the long arm of chromosome 3 (3q29) on the Crick (minus strand). This condition is rare. Only four cases have been described up to 2017.  This syndrome was first described by in Stewart et al. 2007.
