Molecular Plant Pathology is a bimonthly peer-reviewed scientific journal published by Wiley-Blackwell on behalf of the British Society for Plant Pathology. It was established in January 2000 by Prof Gary Foster, University of Bristol, who acted as Editor-in-Chief from 2000 to 2012. The journal covers research concerning plant pathology, in particular its molecular aspects such as plant-pathogen interactions. The editor-in-chief is Marty Dickman (Texas A&M University). According to the Journal Citation Reports, the journal has a 2011 impact factor of 3.899, ranking it 20th out of 190 journals in the category "Plant Sciences".
