The trilogy of Fallot is a congenital heart disease consisting of the following defects: pulmonary valve stenosis, right ventricular hypertrophy and atrial septal defect. However, the tetralogy has a ventricular septal defect instead of an atrial one, and it also involves an overriding aorta. Thoracotomy is used to surgically correct this pathology.  It is named in honor of its discoverer: Etienne Fallot.
