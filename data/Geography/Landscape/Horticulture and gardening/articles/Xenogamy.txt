Xenogamy (Greek xenos=stranger, gamos=marriage) is the transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of a different plant. This is the only type of pollination which during pollination brings genetically different types of pollen grains to the stigma.   The term xenogamy (along with geitonogamy and autogamy) was first suggested by Kerner in 1876.
