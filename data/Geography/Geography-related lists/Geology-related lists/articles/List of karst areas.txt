Karst topography is a geological formation shaped by the dissolution of a layer or layers of soluble bedrock, usually carbonate rock such as limestone or dolomite, but also in gypsum.  It has also been documented for weathering-resistant rocks, such as quartzite, given the right conditions.  This is an incomplete list of the major karst landscape areas of the world.
