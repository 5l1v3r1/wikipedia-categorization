 This is a list of countries and territories formerly ruled or administered by the United Kingdom or part of the British Empire, with their independence days. Some countries did not gain their independence on a single date, therefore the latest day of independence is shown with a break down of dates further down.  Papua New Guinea gained independence from Australia on 16 September 1975. Constitution Act, 1982  Singapore gained independence from Malaysia on 9 August 1965.  Samoa gained independence from New Zealand on 1 January 1962. New Zealand has responsibilities for two freely associated states:  Cook Islands (from 4 August 1965)  Niue (from 19 October 1974)  Bangladesh gained independence from Pakistan in 1971 (recognised in 1972).  Namibia gained independence from South Africa on 21 March 1990. Anguilla declared independence from St Kitts and Nevis in 1967, following a revolution; in favour of returning to British authority in 1971 with full British Crown Colony status (renamed in 2002 as British Overseas Territory status) returning in 1980.
