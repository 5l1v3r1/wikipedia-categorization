Most Iapetian geological features are named after characters and locations in the Old French epic poem The Song of Roland, specifically the English translation by Dorothy L. Sayers. A mons /ˈmɒnz/, pl. montes /ˈmɒntiːz/, is a mountain.  There is one named Iapetian regio /ˈriːdʒioʊ/ (area of distinct albedo difference), Cassini: The adjectival form is Cassinian /kəˈsɪniən/.
