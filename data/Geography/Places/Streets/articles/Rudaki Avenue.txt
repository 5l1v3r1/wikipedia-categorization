Coordinates: 38°35′33.2″N 68°47′12.6″E﻿ / ﻿38.592556°N 68.786833°E﻿ / 38.592556; 68.786833 Rudaki Avenue (Tajik: хиёбони Рӯдакӣ, Russian: проспект Рудаки) is the main thoroughfare street in Dushanbe, the capital of Tajikistan.    The street is named after the Rudaki, the Tajik national poet.  The street was known as Lenin Avenue during the Soviet period, and used to host a statue of V. I. Lenin.  The street received its current name in the summer of 1992.  Key administrative buildings and cultural institutions are located on the street.
