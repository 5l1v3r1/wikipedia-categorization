Coordinates: 36°N 105°E﻿ / ﻿36°N 105°E﻿ / 36; 105 Lanshan mountain range (Ch. 兰山) is a  mountain range along northern shore of the Yellow River east from the capital Lanzhou of the Gansu province in northwestern China. The area of the Lanshan mountains played a prominent role in the history of early China,  changing hands many times in the course of events, after the fall of the Han Dynasty  there was located a capital of a succession of states, in the 4th century it was a capital of the independent state Earlier Liang, and from the 5th to the 11th century it became a center for Buddhist study in China.
