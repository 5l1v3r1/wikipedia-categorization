Johann Georg August Galletti (August 19, 1750 – March 16, 1828) was a German historian and geographer. Galletti was born in Altenburg.  His fame is not based on his merits as an academic, but on his reputation as the creator of a few hundreds of stylistic howlers, becoming a legend as Kathederblüten (teacher's howlers) originated during his time as a grammar school professor in Gotha.
