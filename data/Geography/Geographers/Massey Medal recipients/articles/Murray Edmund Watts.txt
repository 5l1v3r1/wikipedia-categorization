Murray Edmund Watts, CM (1909 – October 8, 1982) was a Canadian mining engineer and prospector. He is known for a series of important ore discoveries in the   Arctic regions, beginning at the age of 22,and continuing through a long career. He was mine superintendent of Canadian Malartic Gold Mines and  general manager of Little Long Lac Gold Mines  (1948–56). In 1962, he founded the international geological and mining consulting firm, Watts, Griffis and McOuat Limited with co-founders Arthur Thomas Griffis and Jack McOuat.
