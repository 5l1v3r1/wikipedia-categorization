The Ark (80°43′S 24°47′W﻿ / ﻿80.717°S 24.783°W﻿ / -80.717; -24.783) is a rock summit, 1,790 m, in the central part of the Read Mountains, in the Shackleton Range in Antarctica.  First mapped in 1957 by the CTAE. The name, given by the United Kingdom Antarctic Place-Names Committee (UK-APC), is descriptive of its shape when viewed from the west.   This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "The Ark" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
