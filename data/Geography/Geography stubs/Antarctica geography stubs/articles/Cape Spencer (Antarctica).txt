Cape Spencer (68°24′S 147°29′E﻿ / ﻿68.400°S 147.483°E﻿ / -68.400; 147.483) is an ice-covered point marking on the east the seaward end of the depression occupied by the Ninnis Glacier, located in George V Land.  It was discovered by the Australasian Antarctic Expedition (1911-14) under Douglas Mawson. He named it in 1911 for Sir Baldwin Spencer, the Director of the National Museum in Melbourne.  This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Cape Spencer (Antarctica)" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
