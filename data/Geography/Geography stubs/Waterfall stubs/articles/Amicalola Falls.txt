Amicalola Falls  is a 729-foot (222 m) waterfall on Amicalola Creek in Dawson County, Georgia, United States.  It the highest waterfall in Georgia and is considered to be one of the Seven Natural Wonders of Georgia.   The name "Amicalola" is derived from a Cherokee language word meaning "tumbling waters".   The falls are the centerpiece of Amicalola Falls State Park.   Media related to Amicalola Falls at Wikimedia Commons
