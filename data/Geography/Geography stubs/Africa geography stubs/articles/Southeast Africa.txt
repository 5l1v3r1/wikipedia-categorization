Southeast Africa   or Southeastern Africa [a] is an African region that is intermediate between East Africa[b] and Southern Africa.[c]  It comprises the countries Botswana, Burundi, Kenya, Lesotho, Malawi,  Mozambique,   Swaziland, Tanzania, Uganda,  Zambia and Zimbabwe  in the mainland, with the island-nation of Madagascar also included.  People include the San people.  The Swahili language is spoken here, both as an official language and lingua franca by millions of people.  Lake Malawi   and Limpopo River  are located here. It is considered that approximately 3,000 years ago, Bantu peoples arrived in this area from what is now Western or Central Africa.  In the 19th and 20th centuries, David Livingstone  and Frederick Courtney Selous visited this area. The latter wrote down his experiences in the book Travel and Adventure in South-East Africa.  Fauna  includes the cheetah, leopard, lion,  Nile crocodile, hyena, Lichtenstein's hartebeest and white rhinoceros.
