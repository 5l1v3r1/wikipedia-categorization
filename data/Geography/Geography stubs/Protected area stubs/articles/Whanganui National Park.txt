The Whanganui National Park is a national park located in the North Island of New Zealand. Established in 1986,  it covers an area of 742 km² bordering the Whanganui River.  It incorporates areas of Crown land, former state forest and a number of former reserves. The river itself is not part of the park.  The park protects the habitat of several thousand North Island brown kiwi, and the also-endangered blue duck. Other birds in the park include the grey warbler, New Zealand pigeon, silvereye, tomtit, tui and whitehead.
