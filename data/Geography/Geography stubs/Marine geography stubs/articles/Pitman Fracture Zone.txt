Pitman Fracture Zone is an undersea fracture zone named for Dr. Walter C. Pitman II, a geophysicist and pioneer in studies of continental drift and seafloor spreading. Name proposed by Drs. Cande, Haxby and Raymond, Lamont-Doherty Geological Observatory [now Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory]. Name approved 3/93 (ACUF 256).  This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Pitman Fracture Zone" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
