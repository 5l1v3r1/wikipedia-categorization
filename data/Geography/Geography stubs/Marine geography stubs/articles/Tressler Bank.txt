Submerged bank Tressler Bank (65°0′S 95°0′E﻿ / ﻿65.000°S 95.000°E﻿ / -65.000; 95.000)  is a submarine bank with a least depth of 56 fathoms, extending from about 94 to 96E in the eastern part of the Davis Sea. The bank was sounded by the USS Burton Island and USS Willis L. Tressler of the U.S. Navy Hydrographic Office who carried on oceanographic studies in the Antarctic aboard the USS Tressler was scientific leader at Wilkes Station in 1958.  This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Tressler Bank" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
