Submerged bank Ives Bank (67°40′S 68°12′W﻿ / ﻿67.667°S 68.200°W﻿ / -67.667; -68.200) is a submarine bank with a least depth of 11 metres (6 fathoms) in the Bellingshausen Sea. It is located in the southern approaches to Ryder Bay, Adelaide Island, Antarctica, 1 mile (1.6 km) south of the Mikkelsen Islands. It was named by the UK Antarctic Place-Names Committee after Lieutenant Commander David M. Ives, Royal Navy, who surveyed this bank from HMS Endurance in March 1981.   This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Ives Bank" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
