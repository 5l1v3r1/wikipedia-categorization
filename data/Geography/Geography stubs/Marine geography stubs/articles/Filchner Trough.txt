The Filchner Trough (77°S 36°W﻿ / ﻿77°S 36°W﻿ / -77; -36Coordinates: 77°S 36°W﻿ / ﻿77°S 36°W﻿ / -77; -36) is an undersea trough extending north from its associated feature, the Filchner Ice Shelf. The name, proposed by Dr. Heinrich Hinze of the Alfred Wegener Institute for Polar and Marine Research, Bremerhaven, Germany, was approved by the Advisory Committee for Undersea Features in June 1997.   This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Filchner Trough" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
