Submerged bank Vinci Bank (77°30′S 34°30′W﻿ / ﻿77.500°S 34.500°W﻿ / -77.500; -34.500) is a submarine bank in the Weddell Sea. It is named after Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519), world-renowned scientist and artist. The name was proposed by Dr. Heinrich Hinze, Alfred Wegener Institute for Polar and Marine Research, Bremerhaven, Germany. Name approved 6/97 (ACUF 271).  This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Vinci Bank" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
