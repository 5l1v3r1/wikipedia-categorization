Endurance Ridge (62°30′S 40°0′W﻿ / ﻿62.500°S 40.000°W﻿ / -62.500; -40.000Coordinates: 62°30′S 40°0′W﻿ / ﻿62.500°S 40.000°W﻿ / -62.500; -40.000) is an Antarctic undersea ridge, south-east of Coronation Island in the South Atlantic Ocean. It was named after the Endurance Expedition of 1914–1917, and its name was approved by the Advisory Committee for Undersea Features in June 1987.    This article incorporates public domain material from the United States Geological Survey document "Endurance Ridge" (content from the Geographic Names Information System).
