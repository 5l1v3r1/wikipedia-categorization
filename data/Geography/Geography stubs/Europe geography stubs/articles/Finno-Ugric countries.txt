Finno-Ugric countries is a term used for the three independent nation states with a Finno-Ugric national majority:  Finnic Finland and Estonia and the Ugric Hungary. The Finno-Ugric countries work together in funding Finno-Ugric research and protecting the minority rights of other Finno-Ugric nations without their own sovereign states.  The three countries are also represented as Finno-Ugric countries in the Finno-Ugric Congress
