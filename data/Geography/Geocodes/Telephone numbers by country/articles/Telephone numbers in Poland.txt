The assignment of telephone numbers in Poland is controlled by the Office of Electronic Communications (UKE), the national regulatory authority. Polish telephone numbers have 9 digits since 30 September 2009.  The prefix '0' is no longer used when making a call within the country.  9 digits, starting with 4, 5, 6, 7 or 8 (not all). For example - 51 646 50 75 The same codes are used by mobile virtual network operators.
