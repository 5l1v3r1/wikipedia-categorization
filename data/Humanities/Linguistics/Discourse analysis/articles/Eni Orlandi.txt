Eni de Lourdes Puccinelli Orlandi is a Brazilian researcher with great influence in French Discourse Analysis, being the one to bring it to Brazil by translating to Portuguese Michel Pêcheux's books and articles. She is a researcher at Laboratório de Estudos Urbanos - Unicamp (Laboratory of Urban Studies of the University of Campinas), professor and coordinator of the Science Program of the Universidade do Vale da Linguagem do Sapucaí and professor of the University of Campinas. She is also a researcher 1A at Conselho Nacional de Desenvolvimento Científico e Tecnológico (CNPq).  Graduated in Arts and Literature from Faculty of Philosophy, Sciences and Letters of Araraquara (1964), has a master's degree in Linguistics from the University of São Paulo (USP) (1970), PhD in Linguistics from USP and University of Paris / Vincennes (1976). She was a professor at USP from 1967 to 1979. From 1971 to 1974 she taught the discipline of discourse analysis in the specialization course in translation at Pontifical Catholic University of Campinas.
