A singular term is a paradigmatic referring device in a language. Singular terms are of philosophical importance for philosophers of language, because they refer to things in the world, and the ability of words to refer calls for scrutiny. Singular terms are defined as expressions that purport to denote or designate particular individual people, places, or other objects. They contrast with general terms (such as "car" or "chair") which can apply to more than one thing.   There are various kinds of singular terms: proper names (eg. "Matthew"), definite descriptions (eg. "the second fisherman in the boat"), singular personal pronouns (eg. "she"), demonstrative pronouns (eg.
