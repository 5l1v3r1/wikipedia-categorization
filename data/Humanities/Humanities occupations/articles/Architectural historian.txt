An architectural historian is a person who studies and writes about the history of architecture, and is regarded as an authority on it.  As many architectural historians are employed at universities and other facilities for post-secondary education, in addition to bachelor's degree, it is normal for colleges and universities to require the PhD degree for new full-time hires and a master's degree for part-timers.
