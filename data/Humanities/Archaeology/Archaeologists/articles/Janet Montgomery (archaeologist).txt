Janet Montgomery is a professor at Durham University and a leading archaeological scientist. Montgomery graduated from the University of Bradford in 1996 with a BSc in Archaeological Science.  Janet was awarded a PhD in 2002. Her thesis was the first application of radiogenic lead and strontium isotope analysis to human remains from archaeological sites in Britain, in order to investigate diet and mobility.  Montgomery's work ranges across archaeological periods, and methodological development. Montgomery collaborates with Jane Evans to undertake the environmental mapping of biosphere strontium in Britain.  Montgomery has received grants from NERC, AHRC, ESRF, Wellcome Trust, British Academy, Royal Anthropological Institute, and the Leverhulme Trust.  She is a member of the Academic Awards Committee of the Executive of the British Federation of Women Graduates.
