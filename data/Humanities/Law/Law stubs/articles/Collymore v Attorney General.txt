Collymore v Attorney General   UKPC 11 is a case of the Privy Council relevant for UK labour law, concerning the right to strike. Collymore and other trade union members for oil companies claimed that a new Industrial Stabilisation Act 1965 in Trinidad and Tobago was ultra vires the constitution, which guaranteed freedom of association.
