 A constitutional convention is a gathering for the purpose of writing a new constitution or revising an existing constitution.   Members of a constitutional convention (sometimes referred to as "delegates" to a constitutional convention) are often, though not necessarily or entirely, elected by popular vote. However, a wholly popularly-elected constitutional convention can also be referred to as a Constituent assembly. Several U.S. states have held multiple conventions over the years to change their particular state's constitutions.
