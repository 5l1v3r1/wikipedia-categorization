Hazelwood House, built in 1882, is a B listed  Arts and Crafts building. It is the largest building in the area known locally as the Glasgow Electric Suburb. It is located on the south side of the city, in the residential suburb of Dumbreck. Situated adjacent to Bellahouston Park, it is located close to the M8 and M77 motorways. Hazelwood House first appear on maps in 1860. In the late 20th century it was used as a nursing home, before being purchased for religious activities and retreats. This building is part of the Hazelwood Conservation Area , which gained this status on 8th March 2002. The house was designed by James Milne Monro . It was later improved by Robert Cumming in 1913. The building has features such as symmetrical front bay windows, a Tudor arch over the front door and a slate roof. It is known as the main building in the 4-street area of Hazelwood estate, Dumbreck known as "Glasgow's Electric Suburb" . This is due to the houses in this area being the first in Glasgow to be built with  indoor electricity- including electric lights and electric cookers.  There is a small lodge house adjacent to it. There are substantial private gardens with a brick walled, earthenware coping and mature trees, The house currently functions as a religious retreat and conference centre.  It holds events annually such as Christmas carols and garden parties which the public can participate in. The public can access the house and the surrounding four streets which comprise the Electric Suburb, on walking tours which take place during Doors Open Days  in Glasgow in September.
