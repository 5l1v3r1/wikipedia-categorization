  The Chief Minister's Northern Territory History Book Award is the premier prize for work pertaining to the history of the Northern Territory of Australia.  The award began in 2004. It was created to recognise "the scholarly, literary and creative achievements" of Australian writers and to encourage the documenting of history of the Northern Territory. The inaugural winner was Dr David Bridgman for "Acclimatisation: architecture at the Top End of Australia.    The award is administered by Northern Territory Library.
