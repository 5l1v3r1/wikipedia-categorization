The Thailand Convention and Exhibition Bureau (TCEB) is a public organization established by royal decree in 2002  to promote Thailand as a destination for meetings, incentives, conferencing, exhibitions (MICE).  The establishment of the organization was announced on 18 September 2002.  It became operational in 2004.  Thailand's MICE revenues in 2004 amounted to 31.7 million baht.  The Royal Thai Government established TCEB under the Office of the Prime Minister to be the organization responsible for stimulating MICE in Thailand). One of the bureau's roles is to encourage the inclusion of Thai arts and culture in these events in order to promote the national heritage.   Chiruit Isarangkun Na Ayuthaya began a four-year term as president of TCEB on 15 May 2017. He is keen on spreading MICE business to areas beyond Bangkok, especially four target cities: Chiang Mai, Khon Kaen, Pattaya, and Phuket.
