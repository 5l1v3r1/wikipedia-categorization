The reveal (also known as the big reveal) is a plot device in narrative structure, and is the exposure to the reader or audience of a previously unseen key character or element of plot or of the performance. A reveal is different from Aristotle's anagnorisis, in which something is revealed to a character rather than to the audience. It may also be used as a device (particularly in the climax) in stage magic by an illusionist or escape artist.
