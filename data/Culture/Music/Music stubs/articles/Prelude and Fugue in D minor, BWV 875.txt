The prelude and fugue in D minor, BWV 875 is a keyboard composition written by Johann Sebastian Bach. It is the sixth prelude and fugue in the second book of The Well-Tempered Clavier, a series of 48 preludes and fugues by the composer.   The prelude has 61 measures and is in 3/4. It is made up of running sixteenth notes and eighth notes.   The fugue has 27 measures and is in common time. It is in 3 voices and has triplets, sixteenth notes, eighth notes, and many accidentals.
