The protocol relating to the status of refugees of 1967 broadens the definition of the Convention Relating to the Status of Refugees of 1951. The main point of the protocol was to "grant all refugees the same status irrespective of the effective date of the 1st of January 1951." The protocol was completed on the 31st of January 1967 in New York City. Switzerland ratified the protocol on the 20th of May 1968.  Germany ratified it on the 5th of November, 1969,  and Austria ratified it on the 5th of September 1973. Directive 2011/95  of the European Parliament, derives from this protocol, among other sources.
