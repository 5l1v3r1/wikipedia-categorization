Lubu people are an ethnic group who live in central Sumatra, Indonesia. They are similar to the Kubu people, and are also ancestral to the Siladang people.  They live in the mountainous regions of Padang, Lawas, Ankola, and Mandheling of southern Batakland.  They are now in the process of being absorbed by the Batak. In the early 20th century, they were a migratory people who lived in tree houses, and now are still a tribal people. Although they live near the rivers, they are fearful of water.   They speak the Lubu language.
