The Mariu were an indigenous Australian people of the Northern Territory. In Norman Tindale's estimation the Mariu's territory covered some 1,800 sq. miles in the area south of where the Victoria River enters the Joseph Bonaparte Gulf. The Bullo River also formed part of their land.   In 1900 R. H.
