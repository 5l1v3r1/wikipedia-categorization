Romani people in Poland (Polish: Romowie, commonly known as Gypsies Polish: Cyganie) are one of Poland's recognized ethnic minorities.  According to the Polish census of 2011, 17049 people in Poland declared themselves as Romani people.  The recorded history of the Romani people in Poland dates to the 15th century. Major ethnic subgroups of Romani people in Poland are: the Polska Roma, the Bergitka (Carpathian) Roma, Kalderash and Lovari.
