Chokri, (also known as Chakru, Chakhesang and Eastern Angami) is one of three languages spoken by the Chakhesang Naga of Phek district, Nagaland state, India.  Cheswezumi is main Chokri village in Nagaland. There are also some Chokri speakers located in Senapati district, Manipur.  In 1991 it was estimated there were 20,000 native Chokri speakers.   The Chokri language is largely written in the Latin script.
