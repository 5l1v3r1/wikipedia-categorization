Richard Slotkin (born 1942) is a cultural critic and historian. He is the Olin Professor of English and American Studies at Wesleyan University in Middletown, CT, and in 2010 is a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.   Slotkin writes novels alongside his historical research, and uses the process of writing the novels to clarify and refine his historical work.  Slotkin received his BA from Brooklyn College, his MAAE from Wesleyan University and his PhD from Brown University.  He started teaching at Wesleyan University in 1966 and helped establish the school's American studies and film studies program.  In 1995 he received the Mary C. Turpie Award of the American Studies Association for his contributions to teaching and program-building.  His novel, Abe: A Novel of the Young Lincoln, won the 2000 Michael Shaara Award for Excellence in Civil War Fiction.
