Italian Americana is a biannual peer-reviewed academic journal covering studies on the Italian-American experience. It publishes history, fiction, memoirs, poetry, and reviews.  The editor-in-chief is Carla A. Simonini (Youngstown State University). The journal was established in 1974 by Richard Gambino, Ernest Falbo, and Bruno Arcudi under the auspices of Queens College, City University of New York, where Gambino taught.  In 1989, Carol Bonomo Albright became editor, and the journal was published by the University of Rhode Island. Other notable editors have included novelist Christine Palamidessi Moore and poets Michael Palma and Maria Terrone. Simonini became editor in 2015, and the journal moved to Youngstown State University.
