The Romance-speaking world,   romanophone,   neolatin world,  or Latin-speaking world,  is the part of the world where Romance languages (those derived from Latin) are either official, co-official, or significantly used, comprising Latin America, Latin Europe, Romance-speaking Africa and Romance-speaking Asia. It includes the Spanish-, Portuguese-, French-, Italian-, Romanian- and Catalan-speaking communities. In European countries like this, the 6-continent combined-America model is usually taught.
