The Jarrakan (formerly Djeragan) languages are a small family of Australian Aboriginal languages spoken in northern Australia. The name is derived from the word jarrak, which means "language" in Kija. Doolboong may also have been a Jarrakan language, but this uncertain as it is extinct and essentially unattested.
