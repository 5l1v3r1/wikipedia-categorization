 Tamagozake (卵酒 or 玉子酒) is a drink consisting of heated sake, sugar and a raw egg.  It translates as "egg sake", being made of the kanji 卵 tamago (egg) and 酒 sake. Tamagozake is a traditional home remedy for the common cold in Japan, however there is no medical proof of its efficacy.  Even though it is an alcoholic drink, it is sometimes given as a cold remedy to children as well as adults, much as hot toddies are used in Western countries.
