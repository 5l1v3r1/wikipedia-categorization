Cox's Bazar Beach, located at Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh, is the longest unbroken sea beach in the world,   running 120 kilometres (75 mi).  It is the top tourist destination of Bangladesh.   At high tide the beach is 200 metres (660 ft) wide and at low tide it is 400 metres (1,300 ft) wide on average.  Quicksand is a danger during ebb tide.
