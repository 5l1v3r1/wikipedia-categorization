The Bandiera Arancione (Orange Flag in Italian) is a recognition of quality awarded by the  Touring Club Italiano to small towns (population 15,000 or less) in Italy for excellency in tourism, hospitality and the environment. This recognition was established in 1998 in Liguria, in response to a regional institution's demand to foster and promote the Italian  hinterland. The Touring Club Italiano (TCI) developed an analytical model to identify the first towns recognized with the 'Bandiera Arancione'. Later the recognition was adopted on nationwide scale to identify sites of excellence in all regions of Italy. This is the only Italian project of its kind to be listed by the World Tourism Organization among successful programs for the sustainable development of tourism around the world.
