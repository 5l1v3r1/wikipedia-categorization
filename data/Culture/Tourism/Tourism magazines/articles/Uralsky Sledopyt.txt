Uralsky Sledopyt (Russian: Уральский Следопыт, Urals Pathfinder) is a Soviet and Russian magazine dedicated to tourism and local history. It also has a science fiction section. It is printed in Yekaterinburg (formerly Sverdlovsk), Russia, located on the eastern side of the Ural mountain range, hence the name of the magazine.  In 1981 the magazine established the Aelita Prize for science fiction.
