 Religious tourism, also commonly referred to as faith tourism , is a type of tourism, where people travel individually or in groups for pilgrimage, missionary, or leisure (fellowship) purposes. The world's largest form of mass religious tourism takes place in India at the Kumbh Mela pilgrimage, which attracts over 100 million pilgrims. North American religious tourists comprise an estimated $10 billion of the industry.  Modern religious tourists are more able to visit holy cities and holy sites around the world.
