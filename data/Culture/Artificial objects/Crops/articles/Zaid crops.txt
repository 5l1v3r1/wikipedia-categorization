In the Indian sub-continent, crops grown on irrigated lands which do not have to wait for monsoons, in the short duration between Rabi and Kharif crop season, mainly from March to June, are "Zaid crops". These crops are mainly grown in the summer season during a period called the "Zaid crop season." They require warm dry weather for major growth period and longer day length for flowering. The main produce are seasonal fruits and vegetables they are sown during the months of March and harvested by June and July  Cucumber Musk melon Watermelon Bitter gourd Pumpkin
