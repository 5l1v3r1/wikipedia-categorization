The Political Economy of Research and Innovation (PERI) (or sometimes political economy of technoscience) is an emerging academic field at the interface of science and technology studies and political economy. It focuses on the production, distribution, and consumption of knowledge, and how these shape and are shaped by different political economies.  Most scholars in this field have so-far focused on the two-way relationship between science, technology, and innovation and political economic processes, practices, and logics.  It has its origins in the critique of neoclassical or orthodox economics of science by scholars like Philip Mirowski,  the 'economic turn' in science and technology studies (see social studies of finance and valuation studies),  and innovation studies or science policy.
