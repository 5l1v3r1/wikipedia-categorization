The American Institute for Stuttering is an American nonprofit organization that provides universally affordable speech therapy to people who stutter. The organization, legally known as The American Institute for Stuttering Treatment and Professional Training, was founded in 1998 by speech-language pathologist Catherine Otto Montgomery in New York, NY. The current clinical director is speech-language pathologist Heather Grossman, PhD.  The organization provides stuttering therapy to children and adults  as well as clinical training to speech-language pathologists seeking specialized knowledge in the treatment of stuttering.
