The Mundart des Kürzungsgebiets is a subdialect of Low Prussian, part of Low German, spoken in today's Poland.  In 1918, it was spoken in East Prussia and West Prussia in their respective then borders.   Mundart des Kürzungsgebiets was spoken around Braniewo and Frombork and had a border to   Natangian, Westkäslausch, Mundart der Elbinger Höhe and Oberländisch.       There was influence of Salzburgers.   The Western border to Elbląg Upland was a border of denominations.     Part of its Southern border was undetermined by political or religious borders.
