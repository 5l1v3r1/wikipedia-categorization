Uncut currency sheets are common numismatics collector's items. They are often sold as souvenirs by issuers. After cutting, usually the banknotes can be used as legal tender.  The Bureau of Engraving and Printing (BEP) has been selling uncut sheets of United States dollars since October 26, 1981.   Uncut American paper money issued prior to that is a rarity.
