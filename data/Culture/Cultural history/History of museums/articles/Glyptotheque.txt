A glyptotheque is a collection of sculptures. It is part of the name of several museums and art galleries.  The designation glyptotheque was coined by the librarian of King Ludwig I of Bavaria, derived from the Ancient Greek verb glyphein (γλύφειν), meaning "cut into stone."
