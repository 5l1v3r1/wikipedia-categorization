Hau is a notion made popular by the French anthropologist Marcel Mauss in his 1925 book The Gift.  Surveying the practice of gifting, he came to the conclusion that it involved belief in a force binding the receiver and giver. The term 'Hau', used by the Maori, became a paradigmatic example for such a view.  Writing at the turn of the century[when?], Mauss relied on limited sources but his analysis has been expanded and refined.  Gift economy
