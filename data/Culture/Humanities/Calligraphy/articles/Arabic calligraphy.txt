Arabic calligraphy is the artistic practice of handwriting and calligraphy based on the Arabic alphabet.  It is known in Arabic as khatt (Arabic: خط‎), derived from the word 'line', 'design', or 'construction'.   Kufic is the oldest form of the Arabic script. Although most Islamic calligraphy is in Arabic and most Arabic calligraphy is Islamic, the two are not identical. Coptic Christian manuscripts in Arabic, for example, may make use of calligraphy.
