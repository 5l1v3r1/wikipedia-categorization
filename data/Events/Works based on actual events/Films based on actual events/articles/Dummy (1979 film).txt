Dummy is a 1979 American made-for-television drama film starring LeVar Burton as Donald Lang and Paul Sorvino as Lowell Meyers, Lang's attorney.   Based on Ernest Tidyman's nonfiction book of the same name, the film dramatizes the life of Lang, an African-American deaf man from Chicago who was acquitted of the murders of two prostitutes.  The film won a Peabody Award and was nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Television Movie.  The film was released on DVD by the Warner Archive Collection on October 2011.
