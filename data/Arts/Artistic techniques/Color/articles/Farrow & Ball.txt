Farrow is an hypercorrected form of Farrar, an occupational surname for a blacksmith or an ironworker, an old name of early Medieval English and French origin, located in Northern England. The name derives from the Middle English and Old French terms "ferrer, ferreor, ferour" (meaning "smith"), derivatives of "fer" (meaning "iron" in French), from the Latin "ferrum".
