The Djerassi Resident Artists Program is an artists and writers residency in Woodside, California.  Sitting on a 583-acre ranch with a 12-sided cattle barn converted into artist studios.  It was co-founded in 1979 by Carl Djerassi, the inventor of the birth control pill, and Diane Middlebrook. The residency is competitive and held at no cost to the artists. The location was a former cattle ranch. Originally a women's residency program, to honor his late daughter Pamela   but later expanded to welcome all genders.  This residency offers a public open house event once a year.
