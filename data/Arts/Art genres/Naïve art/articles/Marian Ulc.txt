Marian Ulc (born Szałas, Poland, 1947) – a Polish sculptor, representing the naive art movement. His works have been displayed outside Poland, for example in Germany and Sweden. He is most famously known for his 1968 work, "The Greatest Zanzibar Man of Cratus", and his 1971 work, "Bouncing Black Babies". To This day, it is unknown whether he lived or died.
