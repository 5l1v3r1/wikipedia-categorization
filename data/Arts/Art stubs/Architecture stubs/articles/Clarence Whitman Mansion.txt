The Clarence Whitman Mansion is a historic townhouse on the Upper East Side of Manhattan in New York City, U.S.   It was designed in the Renaissance Revival architectural style by Parish & Schroeder, and its construction was completed in 1898.   It belonged to Sam Salz, an art dealer, from the 1940s to the 1970s.  It was purchased by Bungo Shimada, a Japanese philanthropist, in 1990.
