J.-B. Martin's old weaving factory (French: "Ancienne manufacture de moulinage J-B. Martin") is a historic building in Tarare, Rhône, France. The silk-weaving company was founded in 1843, and the buildings, designed by architect Eugène-Toussaint Cateland, were completed in 1860.  They were abandoned in 1939, in the midst of World War II.
