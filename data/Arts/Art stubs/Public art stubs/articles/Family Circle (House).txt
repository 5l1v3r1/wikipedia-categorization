Family Circle is a public artwork by the American artist Herbert House, located at 2300 6th Street NW on the Howard University campus in Washington, D.C., United States. Family Circle was surveyed as part of the Smithsonian's Save Outdoor Sculpture! survey in 1994.  Four nude figures of a male, female and two children dance in a circle. The figures are highly polished steel and have no facial features, feet or hands. The dancing figures are on top of a red circular tilted platform.  Herbert House graduated from Illinois State University. He has been credited with creating over 500 works. Semi-retired, he now[when?] resides in Chicago, Illinois. His work is seen in the collections of Illinois State and numerous private collections.  This sculpture was surveyed in 1994 for its condition and it was described as "well maintained".
