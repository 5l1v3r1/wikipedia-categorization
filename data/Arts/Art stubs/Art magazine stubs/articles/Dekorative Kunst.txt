Dekorative Kunst (meaning Decorative Art in English) was a German avant-garde art magazine published from October 1897   to 1929. The magazine promoted the Jugendstil or Art Nouveau style and was founded by Julius Meier-Graefe.  The publisher of the magazine was Alexander Koch.
