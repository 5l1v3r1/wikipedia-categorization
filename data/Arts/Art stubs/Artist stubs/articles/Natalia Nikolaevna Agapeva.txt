Natalia Nikolaevna Agapeva (or Agap'eva-Zakharova; 3 September 1883 â€“ 25 December 1956) was a Russian artist.  She was born in Taganrog, where her father, Nikolai Fyodorovich Agapiev, worked as a teacher. She studied at Tiflis Art College, graduating in 1906.  She studied at the Moscow School of Painting, Sculpture and Architecture, and was a founding member of the "Moscow Salon" in 1910, along with some of her former fellow students  They gave their first exhibition in February 1911, and folded in 1918.  She died in Moscow, aged 73.
