Fiona Graham-Mackay (nee Fiona Margaret Bain born 24 February apparently at an unknown location 1956) is a British painter. She is known for her portraits of the British royal family.  Graham-Mackay attended the Royal College of Art, where she studied under Quentin Blake.  Her work has been featured on BBC, as she paints Andrew Motion.  Graham-Mackay was chosen to paint the first official portrait of the Duchess of Cambridge.  In 2012, she completed her portrait of Prince Michael of Kent.  Graham-Mackay is also known for her portrait of Seamus Heaney, which was unveiled just before his death in 2013.  Other recent portraits done by Graham-Mackay include Robin Knox-Johnston, Lord Carrington and Michael Palin.
