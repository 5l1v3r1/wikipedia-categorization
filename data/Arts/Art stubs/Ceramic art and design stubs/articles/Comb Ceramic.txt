Comb Ceramic or Pit-Comb Ware (in Europe), Jeulmun pottery or Jeulmun vessel (in Korea) is a type of pottery subjected to geometric patterns from the comb-like tool. This type of pottery was widely distributed in the Baltic, Finland, the Volga upstream flow, south Siberia, Lake Baikal, Mongolian Plateau, the Liaodong Peninsula  and the Korean Peninsula.   The oldest Comb Ceramic is found in the remains of Liao civilization - xinglongwa culture (BC 6200 - 5400 BC) - .
