The National Gallery of Victoria Art School, associated with the National Gallery of Victoria, was a private fine arts college founded in 1867.  It was the leading centre for academic art training in Australia until about 1910.  Among its luminaries, the school was headed by  Sir William Dargie in 1946–1953.,  John Brack from 1962–68, and Lenton Parr from 1968 to its absorption into the newly created Victorian College of the Arts.
