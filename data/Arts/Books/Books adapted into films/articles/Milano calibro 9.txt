Milano calibro 9 is a 1969 short story collection by the Italian writer Giorgio Scerbanenco. It contains 22 stories concerned with the underworld of Milan. The book has been translated to French and Spanish.  Stories from book were the basis for the 1972 film Caliber 9, directed by Fernando Di Leo, and the 1976 film Young, Violent, Dangerous, directed by Romolo Guerrieri.
