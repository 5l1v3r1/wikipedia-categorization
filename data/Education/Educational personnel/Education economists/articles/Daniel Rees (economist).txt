Daniel I. Rees is an American economist who currently serves as Professor of Economics at the University of Colorado Denver.  Rees holds the position of Editor-in-Chief of the Economics of Education Review.  His research interests presently include health and labour economics. Daniel I. Rees earned a B.A. from Oberlin College in 1986, followed by a M.S. in economics from the University of Wisconsin, Madison in 1988 and a Ph.D. in economics from Cornell University in 1992. Since graduating from Cornell University, Rees has been a professor at the University of Colorado Denver. Rees has also held visiting appointments at Princeton University's Center for Health and Wellbeing and Queen's University. He has been affiliated with the IZA Institute of Labor Economics since 2011 and has served as Editor-in-Chief of the Economics of Education Review since 2014.  He is currently an associate editor at the Journal of Population Economics and was an associate editor at Economics and Human Biology from 2011-2013. He has published in a variety of peer-reviewed journals, including the American Economic Journal: Applied Economics,The Economic Journal, and The Review of Economics and Statistics.   Daniel I. Rees' research mainly focuses on health economics and labour economics, especially adolescent health, child health, crime, education, and risky behaviour.
