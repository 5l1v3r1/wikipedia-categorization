The American Annals of the Deaf is a peer-reviewed academic journal published quarterly with one annual reference issue.  The journal is published by Gallaudet University Press in Washington, D.C. It was first established in 1847 as the American Annals of the Deaf and Dumb, the journal's name was changed upon the printing of volume 31, issue 4. The journal has been published continuously since its inception, with the exception of a seven year interruption from 1861 to 1868 due to the American Civil War.  The journal is "the official organ of the Council of American Instructors of the Deaf (CAID) and the Conference of Educational Administrators of Schools and Programs for the Deaf (CEASD)."
