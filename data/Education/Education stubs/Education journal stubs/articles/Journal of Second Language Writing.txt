The Journal of Second Language Writing is a peer-reviewed academic journal in the fields of linguistics and language education. Its scope encompasses all aspects of second and foreign language writing, including writing instruction and assessment. It was established in 1992 and is published quarterly by Elsevier. The current editors-in-chief are Icy Lee (The Chinese University of Hong Kong) and Dana Ferris (University of California, Davis). Associate editors are John Bitchener (Auckland University of Technology) and Amanda Kibler (University of Virginia). The founding editors were Ilona Leki (University of Tennessee) and Tony Silva (Purdue University). According to the Journal Citation Reports, the journal had a 2016 impact factor of 1.591, ranking it 25th out of 182 journals in the category "Linguistics" .
