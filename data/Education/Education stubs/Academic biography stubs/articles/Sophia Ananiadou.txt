  Sophia Ananiadou is a British computer scientist. She led the development of the first publicly-funded text mining group in the world, the National Centre for Text Mining (NaCTeM). She is currently employed by the University of Manchester as the director of NaCTeM and also as a professor in the School of Computer Science.  She also works on Facta+, a searchable database which stores huge amounts of data about cancer, based on data-mining from relevant scientific literature.  Ananiadou was a senior lecturer at Manchester Metropolitan University from 1993 to 1999, and a senior lecturer at the School of Computing Science and Engineering, University of Salford from 2000 to 2005.  Sophia Ananiadou has published extensively since 1986.
