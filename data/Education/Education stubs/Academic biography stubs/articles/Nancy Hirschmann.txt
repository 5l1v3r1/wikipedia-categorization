Nancy Hirschmann (born April 25, 1956) is a Professor of Politics  at the University of Pennsylvania; her specialties are the history of political thought, analytical philosophy, feminist theory, disability theory, and the intersection of political theory and public policy. She received an  BA from Smith College and   MA and PhD degrees  from the Johns Hopkins University. She then became a member of the Cornell University faculty  for 12 years before  moving to the University of Pennsylvania  as   Graduate Chair in the Political Science department. She has taught courses on modern political thought, political theory and public policy, feminist political thought, gender and political theory, and contemporary political thought. Her most recent research is on the topic of disability in political theory.
