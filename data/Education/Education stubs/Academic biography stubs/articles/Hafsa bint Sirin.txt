Hafsa bint Sirin (? - 101/719 c.e.)  was a woman and an early scholar of Islam.  She has been called one of the "pioneers in the history of female asceticism in Island".  She lived in Basra, taught numerous students, was known for her piety and knowledge and practical and legal aspects of Islamic traditions, and has been credited with seventeen traditions.   She was a sister of Muhammad ibn Sirin, known for dream interpretation.
