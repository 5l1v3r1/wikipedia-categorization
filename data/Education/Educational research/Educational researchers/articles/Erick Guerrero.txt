Erick Guerrero is an associate professor at the University of Southern California, Suzanne Dworak-Peck School of Social Work with a courtesy appointment at the Marshall School of Business.  Guerrero grew up in Mexico City and currently works and lives in Los Angeles, CA with his wife and daughter.   Guerrero obtained a degree clinical psychology at the Universidad de las Americas in Mexico City and his PhD in social work in management at the University of Chicago. Trained as an organizational and implementation scientist, his program of research focuses on three inter-related areas associated with improving standards of care for vulnerable populations.  Dr. Guerrero would eventually receive the tenure as an Associate Professor from the University of Southern California, Suzanne Dworak-Peck School of Social Work.  Guerrero has been expanding the scope of his research studies to implementation of evidence-based practices in different health care areas. Most recently, Dr. Guerrero heads the team of Integrated Substance Abuse Treatment to Eliminate Disparities for the University of Southern California, Suzanne Dworak-Peck School of Social Work.  He spent 13 years providing domestic violence and substance abuse counseling in Chicago   This experience led him to obtaining a Ph.D. in macro social work, writing more than 50 articles in this area.  He also leads innovative research on organizational capacity to improve delivery of health care for underserved populations.  He has developed the evidence to inform manager's practices to achieve results for clients from ethnic minority backgrounds. His influential scholarship in management and health care has been acknowledged by several organizations.  Dr. Guerrero is one of few scholars in social work management with a highly funded research agenda to train new investigators and conduct large studies in health care.
