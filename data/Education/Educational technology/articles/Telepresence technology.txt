Telepresence technology is a term used by the  National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) to refer to  the combination of satellite technology  with the Internet to broadcast information, including video in real-time from cameras used on its remotely operated vehicle (ROV) on  Okeanos Explorer. Its ROV will be operating working in a deep sea environment. Data from the ROV is transmitted to a hub based on the land, which then send it to scientists and to the public.   This effort of the Okeanos Explorer has been compared to the lunar landing.
