Conar Instruments was a company located in Washington, D.C.. It was an expansion of the National Radio Institute's (NRI) student supply division. The purpose of this division was primarily to supply test equipment to NRI students and graduates.  The name 'Conar' was derived from the first letters of 'COmpany, NAtional Radio.'  Conar was able to enter the market with a complete series of radio-TV test-equipment kits of proven design developed by NRI's technical staff. Article on the RadioMuseum Website
