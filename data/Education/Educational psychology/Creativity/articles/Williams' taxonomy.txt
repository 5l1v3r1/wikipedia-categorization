Williams' taxonomy is a hierarchical arrangement of eight creative thinking skills conceived, developed, and researched by Frank E. Williams, a researcher in educational psychology.   The taxonomy forms the basis of a differentiated instruction curriculum model used particularly with gifted students and in gifted education settings. The first four levels are essentially cognitive (thinking), while the last four levels are affective (feeling) in nature.
