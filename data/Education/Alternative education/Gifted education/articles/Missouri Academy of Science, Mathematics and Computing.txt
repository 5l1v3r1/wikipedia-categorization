The Missouri Academy of Science, Mathematics and Computing (MASMC) is a two-year residential early college entrance program for gifted high school students at Northwest Missouri State University in Maryville, Missouri, replacing the junior and senior years of high school. It provides an opportunity for students with a passion for math and science to get ahead in a college career, earning both a high school diploma and an Associate of Science degree.  The school opened in 2000 and will cease following the Spring 2018 semester after the University said it was no longer financially viable.  It's peak enrollment was 178 in the 2008-2009 school year.
