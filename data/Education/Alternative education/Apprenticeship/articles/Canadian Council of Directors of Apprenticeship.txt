The Canadian Council of Directors of Apprenticeship is  partnership between Canada's federal, provincial and territorial governments. It supports the development of skilled trades in Canada and it manages the Interprovincial Standards Red Seal Program.  Recently, they've been working to harmonize apprenticeship standards across different jurisdictions.  The council itself has no enumerated powers. However; the representatives from each jurisdiction may hold regulatory powers through specific provincial/territorial legislation. The Canadian Council of Directors of Apprenticeship consists of 15 members. One official from each provincial or territorial jurisdiction, who is responsible for the apprenticeship programs in each area, and two representatives from the Federal government: one from the Department of Employment, and one from Social Development Canada. The CCDA runs 2 different awards programs. The Darryl Cruickshank Memorial Award  is awarded every 2 years on even numbered years to someone in industry who "demonstrated inspirational leadership in the promotion and development of apprenticeship training for aspiring skilled tradespersons" The Red Seal Award of Excellence  is awarded every 2 years on odd numbered years to someone in who is "providing outstanding achievement in the development and promotion of apprenticeship training in Canada.
