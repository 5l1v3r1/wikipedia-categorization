The 2010 Imperative is an initiative issued by Architecture 2030 that requires  ecological literacy, largely missing from the curriculum in the United States, to become a key element of design education by 2010, in order to combat global warming and world resource depletion. The 2010 Imperative calls for the following: "Solutions to design problems must engage the environment in a way that dramatically reduces or eliminates the need for fossil fuel." 	design / studio 	history / theory 	materials / technology 	structures / construction 	professional practice / ethics This may be accomplished by implementing innovative sustainable design strategies, generating on-site renewable power and/or purchasing a maximum of 20% renewable energy and/or certified renewable energy credits (RECs, Green tags).
