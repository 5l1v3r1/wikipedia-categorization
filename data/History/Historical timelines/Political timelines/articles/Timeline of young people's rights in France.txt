Already before the Ancien Régime, young people were able and allowed to enlist in the army. The French Revolution, the République, the SI and the 1804 Napoleonic Code (Code civil des Français) enforce the principles stated in the 1789 Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen. One motto, "Liberté, égalité, fraternité", gradually became the official motto of the French state between 1848   and 1946.
