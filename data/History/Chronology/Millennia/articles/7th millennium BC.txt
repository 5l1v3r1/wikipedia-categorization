The 7th millennium BC spanned the years 7000 through 6001 BC. During this time, agriculture spread from Anatolia to the Balkans. World population begins to grow at an exponential pace due to the Neolithic Revolution, reaching perhaps 10 million.  In the agricultural communities of the Middle East, the cow was domesticated and use of pottery became common, spreading to Europe and South Asia, and the first metal (gold and copper) ornaments were made. farming, animal husbandry pottery, metallurgy, wheel circular ditches, henges, megaliths Neolithic religion
