The Stolpersteine in the Lake Constance district lists all Stolpersteine that have been collocated in Friedrichshafen and Überlingen in the Bodenseekreis ("Lake Constance district") in the very South of Germany. Stolpersteine is the German name for stumbling blocks collocated all over Europe by German artist Gunter Demnig. They remember the fate of the Nazi victims being murdered, deported, exiled or driven to suicide. The list is sortable; the basic order follows the alphabet according to the last name of the victim. Both brothers, sister-in-law Jenny and niece Lore-Luise were all deported to Latvia and murdered in Riga. Only Margot Fellheimer, the other niece, could escape to England. She survived. She was the one and only family member ever coming back to Überlingen, briefly in 1946 in American uniform.
