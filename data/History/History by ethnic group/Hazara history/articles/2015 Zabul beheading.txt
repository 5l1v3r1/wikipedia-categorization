The 2015 Zabul beheading refers to the decapitation of 7 Afghan Hazaras on November 9, 2015 in the southern Afghan province of Zabul. Islamic State fighters had taken the seven members of the Hazara ethnic group hostage a month prior in Ghazni and had been holding them in the Arghandab district of Zabul Province. The hostages included four men, two women, and a nine-year-old girl. They were beheaded using razor wire on 9 November 2015 by IS militants.
