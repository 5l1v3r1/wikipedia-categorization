Coordinates: 39°53′33″N 22°26′53″E﻿ / ﻿39.8924°N 22.4481°E﻿ / 39.8924; 22.4481 Condylon or Kondylon or Condylum (Ancient Greek: Κόνδυλον) or Kondylos  was one of the four fortresses which defended the Vale of Tempe in ancient Thessaly.  It was also called Gonno-Condylon, and was one of the towns of the Perrhaebi.  Condylon's site is at a place now called Zesti Vrysi.    This article incorporates text from a publication now in the public domain: Smith, William, ed. (1854–1857). "Condylon".
