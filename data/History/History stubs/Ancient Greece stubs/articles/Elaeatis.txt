Coordinates: 39°15′N 20°30′E﻿ / ﻿39.25°N 20.5°E﻿ / 39.25; 20.5 Elaeatis or Elaiatis (Ancient Greek: Ἐλαιάτις) is the name of a district of ancient Epirus about the mouth of the Acheron river. The district is mentioned by Thucydides. Its chief town was Elaea.  This article incorporates text from a publication now in the public domain: Smith, William, ed. (1854–1857). "Acheron". Dictionary of Greek and Roman Geography. London: John Murray.
