Coordinates: 39°58′04″N 21°56′02″E﻿ / ﻿39.96769°N 21.9338°E﻿ / 39.96769; 21.9338 Mondaea or Mondaia (Ancient Greek: Μονδαία) was a town and polis (city-state) of Perrhaebia in ancient Thessaly.  The city appears in an epigraph dated to 375-350 BCE in a list of Perrhaebian towns that offered a joint dedication to Apollo Pythios.  It also appears in a decree of proxenia of the year 178 BCE in an inscription at Gonnus.   Its location has been found near modern Loutro Elassonos.
