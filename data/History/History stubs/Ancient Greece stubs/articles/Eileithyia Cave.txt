Eileithyia Cave (also the Cave of Eileithyia) was a Neolithic,  Minoan and Mycenaean sacred cave dedicated to the goddess of childbirth, Eileithyia, on the island of Crete. The cave is located one kilometer south of the town of Amnisos. The cave is mentioned in Odysseus's Cretan narrative to Penelope in the Odyssey. The Eileithyia Cave was occupied by prehistoric human settlers from the Neolithic period until around 400 BCE. The archaeological finds made in the cave are on display at the Iraklion Museum and the Archaeological Museum of Iraklion.
