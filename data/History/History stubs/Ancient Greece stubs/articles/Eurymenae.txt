Coordinates: 39°50′10″N 22°47′31″E﻿ / ﻿39.836°N 22.792°E﻿ / 39.836; 22.792 Eurymenae or Eurymenai (Ancient Greek: Εὐρυμεναί or Εὐρυμέναι)  or Erymnae or Erymnai (Ὲρυμναί)  was a town and polis (city-state)  in Magnesia, ancient Thessaly, situated upon the Aegean Sea coast at the foot of Mount Ossa, between Rhizus and Myrae.    Pliny the Elder relates that crowns thrown into a fountain at Eurymenae became stones.  It was destroyed by Lyciscus in the 4th century BCE. The site has been located at a place called Kokkino Nero.
