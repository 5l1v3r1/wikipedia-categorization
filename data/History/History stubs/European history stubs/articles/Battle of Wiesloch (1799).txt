The Battle of Wiesloch (German: Schlacht bei Wiesloch) occurred on 3 December 1799,  during the War of the Second Coalition, part of the French Revolutionary Wars.    Lieutenant Field Marshal Anton Count Sztáray de Nagy-Mihaly commanded the far right wing protecting the main Austrian army in Swabia, under the command of Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen. With the victory at Wiesloch (on 3 December), Sztáray's force drove the French from the right bank of the Rhine and relieved the fortress at Philippsburg.
