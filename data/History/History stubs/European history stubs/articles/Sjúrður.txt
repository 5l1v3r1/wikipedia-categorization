Sjúrður (or Sigurd), was, around the year 1300, head of Shetland and was also official head of state of the Faeroe Islands   Sjúrður was, together with Bishop Erlendur author of the Faeroese Seyðabrævið in 1298 - a document with land use rules for the Faeroe Islands, and the Faeroes' oldest document.
