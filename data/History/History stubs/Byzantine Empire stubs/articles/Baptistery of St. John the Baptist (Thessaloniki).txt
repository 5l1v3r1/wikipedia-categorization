The Baptistery of St. John the Baptist of Thessaloniki,  considered as the oldest early Christian baptistery and assigned to the building complex of five-aisled basilica of the Episcopal fifth century. The baptistery identified at the excavations of the Byzantine church of Hagia Sophia. It consists of a room with platforms and includes a font.
