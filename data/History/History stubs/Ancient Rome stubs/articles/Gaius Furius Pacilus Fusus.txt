Gaius Furius Pacilus Fusus was a Roman statesman who served as consul in 441 BC and censor in 435 BC. He served as consular tribune in 426 BC, and was defeated in a battle against Veii.    This article incorporates text from a publication now in the public domain: Smith, William, ed. (1870). "Pacilus (1)". Dictionary of Greek and Roman Biography and Mythology. 3. p. 80.
