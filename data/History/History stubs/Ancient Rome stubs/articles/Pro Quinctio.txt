The speech Pro Quinctio was given by Marcus Tullius Cicero on behalf of Publius Quintius.  Gaius Quintius and Sextus Naevius, one of the public criers, had been partners, having their chief business in Gallia Narbonensis. Gaius died, and left his brother Publius his heir, between whom and Naevius there arose disputes concerning the division of the property of the partnership.
