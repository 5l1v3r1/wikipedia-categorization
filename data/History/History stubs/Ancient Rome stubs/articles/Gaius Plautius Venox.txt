Gaius Plautius Venox Hypsaeus was a Roman statesman who served as consul in 347 and 341 BC.  C. Plautius, was a colleague of Appius Claudius Crassus Caecus who brought the Appian water into Rome. C. Plautius received the name of Venox (the searcher of springs), on account of his search for the spring source of this water .  (The Water Supply of the City of Rome, Sextus Julius Frontinus, 97, Book 1, Para. 5)
