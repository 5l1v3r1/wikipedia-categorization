Economy of Argentina Peso (currency) Convertibility plan Corralito Cacerolazo 2001 riots Apagón Economic emergency law Debt restructuring  The Argentino was a complementary currency in Argentina announced by then-president Adolfo Rodríguez Saá on December 26, 2001 during the Argentine economic crisis.  It would have circulated alongside the peso and the dollar. He resigned on December 30, 2001 and this plan was never implemented.  The currency was to replace the Patacón, the LECOP and other provincial bonds.
