Sóndor  (possibly from Quechua suntur: congress, meeting)  is an archaeological site in Peru built by the Chanka people.   The main part consists of a temple like structure used for religious and astronomical purposes. It is located in the Apurímac Region, Andahuaylas Province, Pacucha District.
