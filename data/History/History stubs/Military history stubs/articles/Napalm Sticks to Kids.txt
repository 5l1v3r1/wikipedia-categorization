"Napalm Sticks to Kids" is  a call and response running cadence  occasionally used in the U.S. military.  The earliest recorded example is from 1970.  A version containing a third verse is found in the final draft of the script for the movie An Officer and a Gentleman, but only two verses made it into the film. The line of "Dow Chemical don't give a shit" was changed to "Chemical Service don't...", most likely to avoid liability .
