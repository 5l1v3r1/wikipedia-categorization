Radnice (Czech pronunciation: [ˈradɲɪtsɛ]) is a town in the Plzeň Region of the Czech Republic. It lies some 20 km (12 mi) to the northeast from the region capital of Plzeň. Radnice is also the seat of the Municipality with Commissioned Local Authority within the Plzeň Municipality with Extended Competence. In 19th century coal mining developed in vicinity of Radnice igniting industrial development, especially in the territory of Břasy.
