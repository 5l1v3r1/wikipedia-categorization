Americans of Jewish ethnicity or faith have inhabited the Southern United States and have so for centuries.  The first Jew who arrived in what is now the United States was Luis de Carvajal y de la Cueva, a Portuguese-born Spanish conquistador and alleged slave trader , who crossed the Rio Grande from Mexico into Texas. For years, up until the mid-1800s, the largest Jewish community on the North American continent was in Charleston, South Carolina.
