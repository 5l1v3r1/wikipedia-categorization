 All-Ukrainian Political Union Women for the Future (Ukrainian: Всеукраїнське політичне об’єднання "Жінки за майбутнє") is a political party in Ukraine registered on 30 March 2001. The party was created by Lyudmila Kuchma, a wife of the then serving President of Ukraine Leonid Kuchma. Among notable party candidates was Dmytro Firtash. At the legislative elections of 30 March 2002, the party won 2.1% of the popular vote and no seats;  although final poll results had predicted 5% till 6% of the total votes for the party.  At the legislative elections of 26 March 2006, the party was part of the Opposition Bloc "Ne Tak".  The party did not participate in the 2007 elections.
